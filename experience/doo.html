Overview

As part of the Management Trainee Program at Doo Financial, I rotated across three core departments—Client Management, Trading & Dealing, and Financial Operations. This exposure helped me understand how client requirements translate into trading workflows and how those workflows feed into back-office financial processing, reconciliations, and operational controls.

Before moving into the Operations function, I documented the end-to-end internal workflow from scratch and organised it in Confluence. Since Operations oversees and coordinates across teams, understanding how each department works (and what information flows between them) was essential for ensuring smooth operations and consistent execution.

Core Responsibilities (by Department)

Client Management

  • Supported onboarding for individual, corporate, and institutional clients globally, focusing on smooth account setup processes while aligning with regulatory and compliance requirements.
  • Learned the full onboarding cycle from account opening to configuring rebate structures aligned to client needs and improving operational efficiency.
  • Understood how client setup details influence downstream trading configuration and operational handling.

Trading & Dealing

  • Participated in the configuration and maintenance of trading systems (including Product Center and Upper Broker platforms) to support reliable workflows across regions.
  • Provided operational support for testing and integrating new features into trading systems, ensuring alignment between client expectations and broker requirements.
  • Contributed to UAT and production readiness testing to identify potential issues early before full rollout.

Financial Operations

  • Overseen fund-in and fund-out processes, ensuring accurate and timely internal transfers, reconciliations, and fund management for both retail and institutional clients.
  • Managed and updated exchange rates in a multi-currency environment to support compliant and accurate financial transactions.
  • Connected trading outputs to financial processing steps by learning how operational data is structured, validated, and reconciled end-to-end.

Workflow Documentation & Tools

To prepare for the Operations department, I documented the internal workflow end-to-end from scratch in Confluence, capturing how information and documents move between Client Management, Trading & Dealing, and Financial Operations. I also used Power BI to structure views for operational tracking and analysis where appropriate.
